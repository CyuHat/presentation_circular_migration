Visiting migration ↔︎ Transnationalism in Switzerland

Linking mobility patterns to transnational ties

Core Concepts

  • Transnationalism: Simultaneous participation in:
    1. Transnational mobility
    2. Transnational social networks
    3. Transnational attachment

Findings

  1. Contradict the idea that the longer individual stay the less transnational

  2. Transnationality is ressource-dependant: legal capital (EU passport), education capital (Highly skilled)

  3. Reactive transnationalism (triggered by marginalization and discrimination), does not hold for mobility.

Five ideal types of transmigrant

  1. Immobile, one‑time migrants – low moves, high attachment (Southern EU, women).

  2. Former very‑mobile men, now settled – high pre‑moves, low current mobility, low attachment (EU/US, high education).

  3. One‑time migrants now mobile & attached – low pre‑moves, partner/children abroad, high attachment (women, Asia/LatAm).

  4. Cosmopolitan, hyper‑mobile, low attachment – many moves, friends abroad, low attachment (US/UK, high education).

  5. Hyper‑mobile outsiders – many moves, low attachment, low education (Western Balkans, low legal capital).

Why Visiting migration Matters

  • Pre‑migration mobility
    • 54 % moved once‑twice
    • 37 % moved 3‑5 times
    • 7 % moved > 5 times before arriving in Switzerland.
  • Post‑migration mobility
    • 65 % visit origin ≥ once / year
    • 18 % visit monthly or more.
  • Shows generalized mobility beyond the “high‑skill‑expat” narrative.

Key points

  1. Visiting migration is a continuum

  2. Recently arrived migrants are highly mobile

  3. Need for a proper measure of transnationalism
